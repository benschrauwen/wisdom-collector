# Culture diagnosis and change

Use this when a team or organization is low-trust, stuck, or operating with toxic hidden norms.

## Diagnose emotional reality first
- Ask what the group really feels and what it really cares about.
- Look for unspoken rules, incentives, and habits that shape behavior.
- Use open-ended conversations, not just surveys, to surface what is beneath the surface.
- Start with the actual climate before pushing an ideal vision.

## Make the covert overt
- Name the hidden culture so people can talk about it.
- Preserve what people value while being explicit about what must change.
- Build a shared language for the current reality and the desired future.
- Treat norms as the group-level equivalent of implicit learning: they change through repetition, not slogans.

## Model and mobilize the change
- Senior leaders must visibly model the desired behavior.
- Be accessible, listen, and reduce hierarchy where possible.
- Push decisions downward when it builds ownership and accountability.
- Connect the vision to people’s values and dreams so they feel the change, not just understand it.

## Align the system behind the new norm
- Reinforce change through routines, meetings, rewards, roles, and performance management.
- Use stories, rituals, and symbols to make the new behavior memorable.
- Integrate leadership development with real strategic work, not with isolated training.
- Combine top-down sponsorship with broad participation and local ownership.

## Guardrails
- Do not rely on silence, command-and-control, or one-off workshops.
- Do not try to change deep culture by developing isolated individuals while leaving the group norms untouched.
- Do not let warmth become cozy avoidance of standards; keep accountability in the loop.
