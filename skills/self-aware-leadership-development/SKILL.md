# Self-aware leadership development

Use this when a leader is stuck, overconfident, or unclear about what to change next.

## Diagnose the real self
- Gather candid feedback from bosses, peers, subordinates, and others who see the work.
- Treat 360 input as development data, not as a scorecard.
- Look for strengths and gaps together; avoid reducing the person to weaknesses.
- Compare the **real self** with the **ideal self** the person actually wants to become.

## Build a useful learning agenda
- Make the goal personally owned, not borrowed from a boss, mentor, or spouse.
- Build on strengths first.
- Target gaps near the tipping point rather than trying to fix every weakness at once.
- Keep goals feasible, specific enough to practice, and connected to life outside work as well as inside it.

## Practice until it sticks
- Use real situations as the training ground.
- Practice small, repeatable behaviors instead of one-off resolutions.
- Rehearse mentally before difficult moments.
- Expect repetition, feedback, and occasional relapse; emotional habits change through limbic learning, not through insight alone.

## Use relationships to keep learning real
- Work with coaches, mentors, peers, or trusted colleagues who can tell the truth.
- Choose people who offer candor plus psychological safety.
- Reassess periodically, because the ideal self can evolve over time.
